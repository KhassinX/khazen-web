Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 7, 2026

Khazen is built around a simple promise: your money, your data, your rules. This policy explains exactly what information Khazen collects, where it's stored, and what we do with it. We tried to write it in plain English. If anything is unclear, email hello@khassinx.com and we'll explain.

The short version: your financial data lives in your iCloud account, not on our servers. We never sell your data, never show ads, and never use your transactions to train AI models. The only things we keep server-side are minimal technical tokens needed to sync your bank data and notify your devices.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Bank account data (via Plaid)

When you connect a bank account, Khazen uses Plaid to securely retrieve your account information, balances, and transactions. We never see or store your bank login credentials — those go directly between you and Plaid. Plaid passes us back only the data needed to display your accounts: institution name, account type, balance, and transaction history. See Plaid's End User Privacy Policy for what they handle.

1.2 Subscription information

When you subscribe to Khazen Premium, Apple's StoreKit handles the transaction. We receive a subscription status indicator and a transaction identifier from Apple so the app knows your subscription is active. We never see your payment method, name, or billing address.

1.3 Device identifiers

Khazen generates a random anonymous identifier (UUID) on first launch and stores it in your iCloud Keychain. This identifier is used to associate your subscription with your bank connections so we can clean up properly when you cancel. It is never linked to your real identity.

We also receive a push notification token from Apple Push Notification service so we can wake your app when there are new transactions to sync. The token can only be used to send notifications to your device — it does not identify you personally.

1.4 AI provider API keys (Bring Your Own Key)

If you choose to use AI features powered by Anthropic Claude, OpenAI, or Google Gemini, you provide your own API key for those services. The key is stored only on your device in iOS Keychain (with optional iCloud Keychain sync between your own devices). It never reaches our servers. When you ask Khazen's AI a question, the prompt goes directly from your device to your chosen provider.

1.5 Apple Intelligence (on-device)

Khazen uses Apple's on-device Foundation Models for transaction categorization and merchant name cleanup. This happens entirely on your iPhone or iPad — no transaction data is sent to Apple, to us, or to anyone else for AI processing.

1.6 Diagnostic information (only when you explicitly export)

The Settings → DevTools → Diagnostic Dump feature lets you generate a technical report of the app state for support requests. This is generated locally on your device and saved as a file you choose to share. We do not collect crash reports, analytics, or any background telemetry.

2. Where Your Data Lives

  • On your device: all of your financial data (accounts, transactions, budgets, goals) is stored in your local SwiftData database and synced to iCloud.
  • Your iCloud (CloudKit): when iCloud sync is enabled in Khazen Settings, your financial data syncs across your iPhone, iPad, and Mac through your own iCloud account. We have no access to your CloudKit container — only Apple and you do.
  • iOS Keychain: Plaid access tokens, your Khazen anonymous UUID, and any AI provider API keys you've added.
  • Khazen backend (AWS Lambda + DynamoDB, US-East-1): we keep a small amount of technical metadata server-side to make sync work:
    • Plaid access tokens (encrypted in transit, used to fetch your bank data on demand)
    • Device push tokens (so we can wake your app when banks send updates)
    • Subscription state and original transaction ID (for anti-billing-fraud cleanup when you cancel)
    • Item-level health state (which connections need re-authentication)
    We do not store your transactions, balances, or account details on our servers. The Lambda fetches them from Plaid on demand and forwards them to your device, then forgets them.
  • Plaid's servers: Plaid stores your bank credentials and transaction history per their own data retention policy. You can revoke Plaid access at any time from my.plaid.com.

3. How We Use Your Data

We use the information described above only to:

  • Display your financial picture inside the app
  • Sync data across your own Apple devices via your iCloud
  • Notify you when there are new transactions to review
  • Clean up your bank connections when your subscription ends or is refunded (so you're never charged by Plaid for an inactive account)
  • Provide customer support when you contact us

We do not:

  • Sell your data to anyone
  • Show ads inside Khazen
  • Use your transactions to train AI models (ours or anyone else's)
  • Share your data with marketers, data brokers, or analytics providers
  • Track you across other apps or websites

4. Third-Party Services

Khazen relies on a small set of third parties to function. Each handles your data per their own privacy policy:

  • Plaid Inc. — bank account aggregation — plaid.com/legal
  • Apple Inc. — App Store, StoreKit, iCloud, Push Notifications — apple.com/legal/privacy
  • Amazon Web Services — Lambda hosting (US-East-1, Virginia) — aws.amazon.com/privacy
  • Anthropic, OpenAI, Google AI — only if you opt in by providing your own API key. Khazen never holds these keys server-side.

5. Your Rights

You can, at any time:

  • Disconnect a bank from Settings → Connected Banks. This removes the Plaid Item and stops all data fetching for that institution.
  • Delete all your data from Settings → Reset Khazen → Delete My Data. This wipes local + iCloud Khazen data (including the CloudKit zone), removes all Plaid Items so they stop billing, and clears your subscription association from our backend. Apple Guideline 5.1.1(v) compliant.
  • Export your data from Settings → DevTools → Diagnostic Dump (or future Export feature). Provides a structured dump of your transactions, accounts, budgets, and goals.
  • Cancel your subscription from iOS Settings → your Apple ID → Subscriptions. Apple notifies us and we automatically disconnect your Plaid Items so neither Apple nor Plaid keeps charging.

5.1 California Privacy Rights (CCPA / CPRA — 2026 update)

If you are a California resident, you have the following specific rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act):

  • Right to Know — request a copy of the personal information we have collected about you in the last 12 months (or back to January 1, 2022 for data we still retain, per the 2026 enhancement).
  • Right to Delete — request deletion of your personal information. You can do this in-app via Settings → Reset Khazen → Delete My Data, or by emailing us.
  • Right to Correct — request correction of inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale or Sharing — Khazen does not sell or share your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. There is nothing to opt out of, but California law requires us to state this clearly.
  • Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information — your bank account information is sensitive; we use it solely to provide the Khazen service and never for marketing, profiling, or inference.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination — we will not deny you service, charge different prices, or provide a different quality of service for exercising these rights.
  • Right to Opt-Out of Automated Decision-Making — Khazen uses automated categorization (Plaid PFC + on-device Apple Intelligence) for transaction labels and spending insights. These decisions are advisory only, never binding, never reported to third parties, and you can override any category manually. You may also opt out of AI-assisted categorization in Settings → Inteligencia.

Opt-Out Preference Signals (OPTS / Global Privacy Control): Effective January 1, 2026, California honors browser-level opt-out signals such as Global Privacy Control. Khazen is a native mobile app and does not engage in tracking that would trigger OPTS, but we honor any explicit opt-out signal from your device or browser when relevant.

To exercise any California right, email hello@khassinx.com with the subject line "California Privacy Rights Request" and we will respond within 45 days (extendable by 45 days with notice, per CCPA).

5.2 European / UK Privacy Rights (GDPR / UK GDPR)

If you are in the European Economic Area (EEA), the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have the following rights under the EU General Data Protection Regulation:

  • Access — request a copy of your personal data (Art. 15 GDPR)
  • Rectification — correct inaccurate data (Art. 16)
  • Erasure / "Right to be forgotten" — request deletion (Art. 17). The in-app Delete My Data flow fulfills this right automatically.
  • Restriction — limit processing (Art. 18)
  • Data Portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format (Art. 20). The in-app Export feature fulfills this right.
  • Object to processing based on legitimate interests (Art. 21)
  • Lodge a complaint with your national supervisory authority (Art. 77)

Legal basis for processing: we process your data based on (a) the contract we have with you to provide the Khazen service (GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)), (b) your consent when you connect a bank or enable AI features (Art. 6(1)(a) / Art. 9(2)(a) for sensitive financial data), and (c) our legitimate interests in fraud prevention and service security (Art. 6(1)(f)).

Email hello@khassinx.com to exercise any GDPR right. We will respond within 30 days.

5.3 Open Banking Rights (CFPB Section 1033)

Under the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau's Section 1033 final rule, when you connect a bank account through Plaid:

  • Authorization — you explicitly authorize what data Khazen can access through Plaid Link's consent screen.
  • Revocation — you can revoke Khazen's access at any time by tapping Disconnect in Settings → Connected Banks. This is sent to Plaid and propagates to your bank within minutes.
  • Reauthorization every 12 months — Section 1033 requires you to re-authorize each bank connection annually. Khazen will prompt you when reauthorization is needed and use Plaid's Update Mode to streamline the flow without losing your data.
  • Data minimization — Plaid only shares the minimum scopes Khazen needs (account info, balances, transactions). We do not request authentication credentials, identity documents, or any data that is not directly used in the app.

5.4 Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA Safeguards Rule)

Khazen handles "nonpublic personal information" (NPI) as defined under GLBA. We comply with the GLBA Safeguards Rule by maintaining administrative, technical, and physical safeguards including: TLS 1.2+ in transit, encryption at rest of access tokens, principle of least privilege for backend access, regular security audits, and an incident response plan. Our third-party processors (Plaid, Apple, AWS) are also GLBA-compliant.

6. Data Retention

When you delete your data through the app, we remove your record from our backend within 24 hours. Plaid retains some information per their own retention policy; you can request deletion directly with Plaid at my.plaid.com. Backup snapshots of our backend may retain historical data for up to 30 days for disaster recovery purposes, after which it is permanently deleted.

7. Security

All communication between the Khazen app, our backend, Plaid, and Apple is encrypted in transit using TLS 1.2+. Plaid access tokens stored in our backend are encrypted at rest. We don't store passwords, payment methods, or government IDs anywhere — those stay between you and the institutions that issued them.

8. International Data Transfers

Khazen's backend infrastructure (AWS Lambda + DynamoDB) is located in the United States (US-East-1 region, Northern Virginia). If you access Khazen from outside the United States, your information will be transferred to and processed in the U.S. By using Khazen, you consent to this transfer.

For users in the EEA, UK, and Switzerland, we rely on the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) for international transfers, where applicable, with our processors. Our key sub-processors operate under the following frameworks:

  • Apple Inc. — Standard Contractual Clauses + Data Privacy Framework
  • Amazon Web Services — Standard Contractual Clauses + EU Data Boundary commitments
  • Plaid Inc. / Plaid Financial Ltd. (UK) — Standard Contractual Clauses + UK FCA registered Account Information Service Provider

9. Apple Privacy Nutrition Label Alignment

  • Data NOT linked to you (not used to track you):
    • Diagnostics — only when you explicitly export a diagnostic dump for support

10. Children's Privacy

Khazen is intended for users 18 years and older. We do not knowingly collect information from anyone under 18. If you believe a child has provided us with information, contact us and we'll delete it.
