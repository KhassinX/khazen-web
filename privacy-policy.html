Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 6, 2026

Khazen is built around a simple promise: your money, your data, your rules. This policy explains exactly what information Khazen collects, where it's stored, and what we do with it. We tried to write it in plain English. If anything is unclear, email hello@khassinx.com and we'll explain.

The short version: your financial data lives in your iCloud account, not on our servers. We never sell your data, never show ads, and never use your transactions to train AI models. The only things we keep server-side are minimal technical tokens needed to sync your bank data and notify your devices.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Bank account data (via Plaid)

When you connect a bank account, Khazen uses Plaid to securely retrieve your account information, balances, and transactions. We never see or store your bank login credentials — those go directly between you and Plaid. Plaid passes us back only the data needed to display your accounts: institution name, account type, balance, and transaction history. See Plaid's End User Privacy Policy for what they handle.

1.2 Subscription information

When you subscribe to Khazen Premium, Apple's StoreKit handles the transaction. We receive a subscription status indicator and a transaction identifier from Apple so the app knows your subscription is active. We never see your payment method, name, or billing address.

1.3 Device identifiers

Khazen generates a random anonymous identifier (UUID) on first launch and stores it in your iCloud Keychain. This identifier is used to associate your subscription with your bank connections so we can clean up properly when you cancel. It is never linked to your real identity.

We also receive a push notification token from Apple Push Notification service so we can wake your app when there are new transactions to sync. The token can only be used to send notifications to your device — it does not identify you personally.

1.4 AI provider API keys (Bring Your Own Key)

If you choose to use AI features powered by Anthropic Claude, OpenAI, or Google Gemini, you provide your own API key for those services. The key is stored only on your device in iOS Keychain (with optional iCloud Keychain sync between your own devices). It never reaches our servers. When you ask Khazen's AI a question, the prompt goes directly from your device to your chosen provider.

1.5 Apple Intelligence (on-device)

Khazen uses Apple's on-device Foundation Models for transaction categorization and merchant name cleanup. This happens entirely on your iPhone or iPad — no transaction data is sent to Apple, to us, or to anyone else for AI processing.

1.6 Diagnostic information (only when you explicitly export)

The Settings → DevTools → Diagnostic Dump feature lets you generate a technical report of the app state for support requests. This is generated locally on your device and saved as a file you choose to share. We do not collect crash reports, analytics, or any background telemetry.

2. Where Your Data Lives

  • On your device: all of your financial data (accounts, transactions, budgets, goals) is stored in your local SwiftData database and synced to iCloud.
  • Your iCloud (CloudKit): when iCloud sync is enabled in Khazen Settings, your financial data syncs across your iPhone, iPad, and Mac through your own iCloud account. We have no access to your CloudKit container — only Apple and you do.
  • iOS Keychain: Plaid access tokens, your Khazen anonymous UUID, and any AI provider API keys you've added.
  • Khazen backend (AWS Lambda + DynamoDB, US-East-1): we keep a small amount of technical metadata server-side to make sync work:
    • Plaid access tokens (encrypted in transit, used to fetch your bank data on demand)
    • Device push tokens (so we can wake your app when banks send updates)
    • Subscription state and original transaction ID (for anti-billing-fraud cleanup when you cancel)
    • Item-level health state (which connections need re-authentication)
    We do not store your transactions, balances, or account details on our servers. The Lambda fetches them from Plaid on demand and forwards them to your device, then forgets them.
  • Plaid's servers: Plaid stores your bank credentials and transaction history per their own data retention policy. You can revoke Plaid access at any time from my.plaid.com.

3. How We Use Your Data

We use the information described above only to:

  • Display your financial picture inside the app
  • Sync data across your own Apple devices via your iCloud
  • Notify you when there are new transactions to review
  • Clean up your bank connections when your subscription ends or is refunded (so you're never charged by Plaid for an inactive account)
  • Provide customer support when you contact us

We do not:

  • Sell your data to anyone
  • Show ads inside Khazen
  • Use your transactions to train AI models (ours or anyone else's)
  • Share your data with marketers, data brokers, or analytics providers
  • Track you across other apps or websites

4. Third-Party Services

Khazen relies on a small set of third parties to function. Each handles your data per their own privacy policy:

  • Plaid Inc. — bank account aggregation — plaid.com/legal
  • Apple Inc. — App Store, StoreKit, iCloud, Push Notifications — apple.com/legal/privacy
  • Amazon Web Services — Lambda hosting (US-East-1, Virginia) — aws.amazon.com/privacy
  • Anthropic, OpenAI, Google AI — only if you opt in by providing your own API key. Khazen never holds these keys server-side.

5. Your Rights

You can, at any time:

  • Disconnect a bank from Settings → Connected Banks. This removes the Plaid Item and stops all data fetching for that institution.
  • Delete all your data from Settings → Delete My Data. This wipes local + iCloud Khazen data, removes all Plaid Items, and clears your subscription association from our backend.
  • Export your data from Settings → Export. Provides a JSON dump of all your transactions, accounts, budgets, and goals.
  • Cancel your subscription from iOS Settings → your Apple ID → Subscriptions. We're notified by Apple and clean up automatically.

If you're in the EU/UK (GDPR), California (CCPA), or any region with similar privacy laws, you also have the right to request a copy of your data, ask for corrections, or object to processing. Email hello@khassinx.com and we'll respond within 30 days.

6. Data Retention

When you delete your data through the app, we remove your record from our backend within 24 hours. Plaid retains some information per their own retention policy; you can request deletion directly with Plaid at my.plaid.com. Backup snapshots of our backend may retain historical data for up to 30 days for disaster recovery purposes, after which it is permanently deleted.

7. Security

All communication between the Khazen app, our backend, Plaid, and Apple is encrypted in transit using TLS 1.2+. Plaid access tokens stored in our backend are encrypted at rest. We don't store passwords, payment methods, or government IDs anywhere — those stay between you and the institutions that issued them.

8. Children's Privacy

Khazen is intended for users 18 years and older. We do not knowingly collect information from anyone under 18. If you believe a child has provided us with information, contact us and we'll delete it.

9. Changes to This Policy

If we change this policy, we'll update the "Last updated" date at the top and, if the changes are material, notify you in the app or by email. The current version always lives at this URL.

10. Contact
